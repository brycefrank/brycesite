Measuring forests

I am a PhD candidate at Oregon State University studying forest biometrics. My particular focus is on large scale forest resource assessment methods that leverage remote sensing data.

Remote sensing data, like lidar data acquired from aircraft, provide information about the forest, but we must rely on statistical inference to make conclusions about its actual state and to assess the uncertainty of our predictions.

These concepts of prediction, actual state, and uncertainty are the central themes of my work in forest biometrics.
